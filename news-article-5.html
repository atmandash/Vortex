Updated Guidelines: New Standards for Sepsis Care

July 15, 2025 By Clinical Policy Team 5 min read

GENEVA (July 15, 2025) â€” The International Sepsis Forum has released its updated "S3 Guidelines" for the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of sepsis, replacing the 2021 protocols.

The new 2025 update includes 29 new recommendations and 16 revised ones. The most significant shift is a move away from rigid "bundles" of care (e.g., everyone gets 30ml/kg of fluid) toward more individualized hemodynamics.

Key Changes

  • Rapid Antibiotics: Confirmation that delays in antibiotic administration >1 hour significantly increase mortality in septic shock.
  • Fluid Resuscitation: A caution against fluid overload; suggesting smaller, more frequent checks of fluid responsiveness.
  • Follow-Up Care: Establishing mandatory post-discharge clinics for sepsis survivors to screen for cognitive decline.

Hospitals are expected to integrate these new standards into their electronic health record (EHR) prompts by the end of the year.

References

  • Evans L, Rhodes A, Alhazzani W, et al. Surviving Sepsis Campaign: International Guidelines for Management of Sepsis and Septic Shock 2021. Intensive Care Med. 2021;47(11):1181-1247.
  • Rhodes A, Evans LE, Alhazzani W, et al. Surviving Sepsis Campaign: International Guidelines for Management of Sepsis and Septic Shock: 2016 Update. Intensive Care Med. 2017;43(3):304-377.
