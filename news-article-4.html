World Sepsis Congress 2025 Highlights

Sep 08, 2025 By Global Coordinator 4 min read

BERLIN (Sep 08, 2025) — The 4th World Sepsis Congress concluded today with a unified call for "Personalized Sepsis Medicine." Over 15,000 delegates from 120 countries attended virtually and in-person.

"The era of treating all sepsis patients with the exact same fluid and steroid protocols is ending," declared Dr. Hans Müller, Chair of the Global Sepsis Alliance. "We are moving towards 'endotyping'—identifying the specific biological sub-type of sepsis a patient has."

Key Takeaways

  • Endotypes: Researchers presented data on four distinct sepsis "endotypes" (e.g., inflammatory vs. immunosuppressive) that respond differently to treatments.
  • Rapid Diagnostics: A focus on point-of-care testing that can identify pathogens in minutes, not days.
  • Survivorship: A new global initiative was launched to support the millions of sepsis survivors suffering from "Post-Sepsis Syndrome."

The Congress ended with a commitment from G7 health ministers to increase funding for sepsis research by 15% over the next two years.
