Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is sepsis?

Sepsis is the body's extreme response to an infection. It happens when an infection you already have triggers a chain reaction throughout your body. Without timely treatment, it can lead to tissue damage, organ failure, and death.

Who is most at risk of getting sepsis?

Anyone can get sepsis, but risk is higher for adults 65+, children younger than 1, people with weakened immune systems, and those with chronic medical conditions (like diabetes, lung disease, cancer, or kidney disease).

What are the first signs I should look out for?

Common signs include high heart rate, low blood pressure, fever or shivering, confusion/disorientation, shortness of breath, and extreme pain or sweaty skin. "TIME" is a helpful acronym: Temperature, Infection, Mental decline, Extremely ill.

How accurate is this screening tool?

This tool uses clinically validated scoring systems (like qSOFA) to estimate risk. However, it is not a diagnostic tool. It provides a preliminary risk assessment to help you decide if you need immediate medical attention.

Can I use this tool for my child?

This specific screening questionnaire is optimized for adults. Sepsis symptoms in children can appear differently (e.g., skin feeling cold, no urine for 12 hours). Please consult a pediatrician immediately if you suspect your child is unwell.

Yes, the sepsis risk screening tool is 100% free to use for everyone. Our goal is to raise awareness and help save lives through early detection.

What can I do to prevent sepsis in the first place?

The best prevention is preventing infections. Wash your hands frequently, keep cuts clean and covered, keep up with vaccinations (flu, pneumonia, etc.), and manage chronic conditions carefully.
