MEDICAL EMERGENCY

What is Sepsis?

Sepsis is the body's extreme response to an infection. It is a life-threatening medical emergency. Without timely treatment, sepsis can rapidly lead to tissue damage, organ failure, and death.

Know the Signs:
TIME Matters

  • Temperature

    Higher or lower than normal.

  • Infection

    Signs of infection (pneumonia, UTI, etc).

  • Mental Decline

    Confused, sleepy, or difficult to rouse.

  • Extremely Ill

    Feeling "worse than ever" or severe pain.

Clinical Criteria (qSOFA)

Doctors use the qSOFA score to quickly identify high-risk patients:

Respiratory Rate
≥ 22 breaths/min
Altered Mental Status
GCS < 15
Systolic Blood Pressure
≤ 100 mmHg

Suspect Sepsis? Act Fast.

For every hour treatment is delayed, the risk of death increases by 8%.
