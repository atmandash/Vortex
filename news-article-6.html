Study Find: Continuous Antibiotic Infusion Saves Lives

Aug 20, 2025 By Research Desk 3 min read

SYDNEY (Aug 20, 2025) — A massive clinical trial involving over 7,000 patients has concluded that a simple change in how antibiotics are delivered could save thousands of lives annually.

The study, published in the *Journal of Critical Care*, compared the standard "intermittent" method (giving a dose every 4-6 hours) with "continuous infusion" (a slow, steady drip over 24 hours).

Why It Works

Certain antibiotics, like penicillin, are more effective when their concentration in the blood remains constant, rather than spiking and dropping. This "time-dependent killing" is crucial for tackling the drug-resistant bacteria often found in ICU sepsis cases.

"We saw a reduction in mortality of nearly 25% in the continuous infusion group," stated Prof. Mark Davis. "It requires no new drugs, no expensive equipment—just a change in the pump settings."

Hospitals in Australia and the UK are already updating their protocols to make continuous infusion the default standard of care for beta-lactam antibiotics.

References
