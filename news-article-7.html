AI Model "COMPOSER" Reduces Sepsis Mortality by 17% at UC San Diego Health

Jan 12, 2026 By Clinical Research Dept 5 min read

COMPOSER AI Engine

Continuous Monitoring of 150+ Clinical Variables

SAN DIEGO (Jan 12, 2026) â€” In a landmark study published this week, UC San Diego Health revealed that its deep-learning AI model, known as COMPOSER, has successfully reduced in-hospital sepsis mortality by a staggering 17%.

The model, which has been integrated into the clinical workflow across emergency departments and intensive care units, continuously monitors more than 150 patient variables in real-time. This includes vital signs, laboratory results, nursing notes, and demographics, allowing for detection hours before traditional clinical signs manifest.

Early Detection, Faster Intervention

The primary challenge in sepsis treatment is timing. For every hour that treatment is delayed, the risk of mortality increases significantly. COMPOSER works by analyzing patterns that are often too subtle for the human eye, providing clinicians with a "high-risk" alert that prompts immediate evaluation.

"AI doesn't replace the doctor; it acts as a tireless guardian," says Dr. Gabriel Wardi, chief of the Division of Critical Care. "By the time a patient's blood pressure drops, they are often already in septic shock. COMPOSER finds them before that cliff."

The Power of Data Integration

What sets COMPOSER apart is its ability to handle unstructured data. Unlike older rule-based systems (like simple qSOFA calculations), COMPOSER uses advanced neural networks to understand the context of a patient's entire medical history and current trajectory.

Since its implementation, the model has analyzed over 100,000 patient encounters, demonstrating high sensitivity and a low false-alarm rate, which is critical for preventing "alert fatigue" among nursing staff.

Key Study Impact:

  • 17% reduction in absolute sepsis mortality.
  • 12% improvement in bundle compliance (timely antibiotics and fluids).
  • Reduced ICU length of stay by average of 14 hours.

As healthcare systems globally look to integrate AI, the success of the COMPOSER model at UC San Diego Health serves as a blueprint for how machine learning can be safely and effectively deployed to save lives at the bedside.
